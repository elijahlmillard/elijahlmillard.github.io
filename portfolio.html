REEL 01 // ABOUT

ELIJAH MILLARD

DEVELOPER - KANSAS CITY

Some people find their calling chasing what's new. I found mine in what endures.

I'm Eli - a self-directed developer completing the i.c.stars technology internship in Kansas City. My path here wasn't traditional. I was homeschooled, with a father who's a software engineer and the sense to let me build a curriculum around what actually fascinated me: systems, constraints, and the satisfaction of making something work that wasn't supposed to.

That started with an Arduboy - a handheld console you program in C. Scarce documentation, tight hardware limits, a language pushed well past its comfort zone. Getting a game running on it felt like cracking a code that wasn't meant to be cracked. I got hooked on that feeling. It led me to COBOL - not because it's trendy, but because legacy systems have that same character. Deep constraints, decades of embedded logic, and a shortage of people who can actually read them. I'm currently pursuing COBOL certification alongside AWS Cloud Architect, because understanding where these systems are going matters as much as knowing where they've been.

Outside the terminal, I'm an Eagle Scout with Troop 391, a three-year scout camp staffer, a bonsai grower, and someone who thinks a well-ordered space reflects a well-ordered mind.

PROJECTS

KCPS CONTENT HUB

A SharePoint-based knowledge base developed for Kansas City Public Schools to reduce internal support tickets by centralizing employee resources. Built custom web parts using TypeScript, React, and the SharePoint Framework (SPFx) for direct deployment to SharePoint pages.

COBOL PROGRAMS — PUB400

Currently developing and running COBOL programs live on PUB400 under library ELIJAHM1. Focused on understanding the fundamentals of legacy enterprise programming — data structures, file handling, and the architecture that still powers a significant portion of the world's critical infrastructure.

ARDUBOY DEVELOPMENT

Programming games and applications for the Arduboy — a credit card sized open source game system built on Arduino architecture. Working in C++ to build constrained, efficient programs within tight memory and processing limitations. A study in doing more with less.

THIS PORTFOLIO

Built from scratch in vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. No frameworks, no libraries, no build tools. Features an animated 1895-era silent film intro sequence, canvas-based film grain running at 24fps, and a 35mm film strip navigation system. Hosted on GitHub Pages.

LEADERSHIP

Throughout the i.c.stars program, leadership has meant showing up consistently for the team, taking ownership of communication, and stepping into roles that required both accountability and adaptability.

As client liaison on the KCPS Content Hub project, the responsibility was to bridge the gap between what the client needed and what the development team could build — translating business requirements into technical direction and managing expectations on both sides.

Key leadership experiences include Team Week, community roles, giving and receiving feedback workshops, and serving as Tea Host — all of which reinforced the value of active listening, clear communication, and leading through service rather than authority.

BUSINESS

The i.c.stars program operates as a consulting environment. Cohort members form consulting companies, respond to RFPs, and deliver real solutions to real clients under professional conditions.

The KCPS Content Hub project presented a genuine business problem — employees across the district lacked a centralized knowledge base, resulting in high volumes of internal support tickets. The solution required understanding the client's infrastructure, their existing SharePoint environment, and the communication gaps driving the problem.

Company Engagement Days with partners including Accenture and Salesforce provided direct exposure to enterprise consulting culture, professional communication standards, and the expectations of technology employers in the Kansas City market.
